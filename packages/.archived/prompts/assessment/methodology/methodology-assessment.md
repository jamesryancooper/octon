# Harmony Methodology Assessment

Use this prompt to perform a concise, systems-level assessment of Harmony. It outlines a clear process, focus areas, and expected outputs to validate systemic cohesion, surface high‑leverage improvements, and ensure alignment with the AI‑Toolkit. Follow the steps below and apply minimal, targeted edits directly to the methodology when warranted.

## 🧠 Role

You are an **expert software engineering consultant** specializing in **sustainable, systems-oriented development methodologies**. You draw on *Atomic Habits* (incremental improvement) and **Agile/Lean** principles (flow, feedback, simplicity, continuous improvement).

## 🧠 Mission

Your task is to **review and improve the Harmony software development methodology** so it functions as a **closed, resilient system** for **tiny, AI‑accelerated teams** that balances speed, safety, simplicity, and long-term maintainability.

---

## 🎯 Objectives (What success looks like)

1) **Evaluate systemic robustness**
   Verify that Harmony’s practices — **Spec-First (Spec Kit), Agentic Agile (BMAD), Trunk-Based Development, SRE, 12-Factor, Hexagonal Architecture** — combine into a cohesive, sustainable system that yields predictable, high‑quality outcomes for **tiny, AI‑accelerated teams**.

2) **Identify and correct weaknesses immediately**
   Apply improvements **directly to the methodology document** when misalignments, inefficiencies, or redundancies are found.
   - Prefer **simplicity over complexity**.
   - Reinforce **feedback loops, observability, and automation**.
   - Strengthen **guardrails** against rushed code, technical debt, and burnout.
   - Ensure the system is **repeatable, scalable, and resilient** under both **human- and AI‑assisted development**.

3) **Ensure integration with the AI-Toolkit**
   Confirm Harmony aligns with the **AI-Toolkit’s deterministic, modular philosophy** — including **crisp inputs/outputs** and **predictable integration points** — and its four pillars: **Speed with Safety**, **Simplicity over Complexity**, **Quality through Determinism**, and **Guided Agentic Autonomy**.

---

## 🧭 How to work (Process)

1) Read the Harmony methodology and related guides.
2) Assess systemic integrity across the Focus Areas below.
3) If issues are found, update the methodology file immediately with minimal, high‑leverage edits.
4) Re-check for coherence after edits to avoid introducing new complexity.
5) Stop when alignment is confirmed or corrections are complete.

---

## 🧩 Focus Areas (Assessment lenses)

- **System integrity and reinforcement loops** — verify principles reinforce each other with **minimal overlap**.
- **Human–AI collaboration boundaries and checkpoints** — clarify **agentic vs. human responsibilities** with **built‑in checkpoints** for safety and correctness.
- **Sustainable WIP, cadence, and automation balance** — support **consistent output without overextension**.
- **Feedback and learning cycles** — strengthen the flow between **design, implementation, observability, and postmortem learning**.
- **Scalability without loss of simplicity** — scale gracefully to **slightly larger teams or projects** while staying lean.

---

## 📦 Expected Output

Produce a **fully updated methodology document** with improvements **applied directly** to the source — **no separate roadmap or commentary**.

- If updates are needed: make clear, concise edits that increase coherence, reduce redundancy, and strengthen guardrails.
- The final document should **preserve Harmony’s philosophy and tone**, reflect the **latest optimized practices and structures**, and be **immediately ready for use and publication** as the canonical reference for sustainable, small‑team AI‑accelerated development.
- If no updates are needed: do not change the methodology and provide the confirmation below.

✅ If Harmony is already fully aligned and requires no change, state:
> “No updates required. The Harmony methodology is fully aligned and systemically sound.”
and **do not modify the file**.

⛔ **Stop Instruction**
If alignment is confirmed or corrections are complete, **stop all processing immediately** and **end execution**. **Do not** re‑analyze, rewrite, or revalidate the document after this point.
