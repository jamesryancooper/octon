# Executive Remediation Charter

## Exact Target State
Octon may honestly claim an **unqualifiedly complete Unified Execution Constitution** only when all of the following are true simultaneously:

1. The constitutional kernel is singular and supreme in practice, not just in prose.
2. Support-target semantics are single-sourced, coherent, and runtime-backed.
3. Authority resolves only through canonical control families.
4. Run lifecycle artifacts are canonically normalized and durably resumable.
5. Disclosure is claim-calibrated and free of stale bounded-envelope language.
6. Closure validators catch all known blocker classes before claim publication.
7. The active release claim is generated from evidence and cannot overstate it.

## Exact Blocker Set
- **A** — Support-target tuple inconsistency
- **B** — Canonical authority leakage into compatibility files
- **C** — Stale claim-calibration wording inside live retained evidence
- **D** — Stage-attempt schema-family / version skew or insufficient proof of normalization
- **E** — Closure-validator underreach

## Why Each Blocker Invalidates an Unqualified Complete Verdict
### A
A repo cannot truthfully claim a universal admitted live support universe while its own support tuple semantics disagree across declaration, admission, projection, and retained runtime evidence.

### B
No live authority artifact may depend on a demoted compatibility surface if the repo simultaneously claims that the normalized directory family is the canonical authority consumer.

### C
Disclosure cannot overstate or understate the active claim. Operational artifacts may not carry stale envelope language that conflicts with the active release scope.

### D
Durable run semantics are constitutional, not stylistic. Active claim-bearing runtime artifacts cannot tolerate schema ambiguity or under-validated family drift.

### E
A closure regime that cannot reliably fail on known blocker classes cannot certify a complete constitutional claim honestly.

## Cutover Doctrine
- The remediation is **atomic**: support-target normalization, authority-family purity, runtime/evidence normalization, disclosure regeneration, and validator hardening land as one integrated cutover set.
- The remediation is **clean-break**: no compatibility aggregate remains in a live canonical control root after cutover; no authored support-target duplicate matrix remains after cutover; no stale claim-envelope wording remains in active claim-bearing artifacts after cutover.
- The remediation is **fail-closed**: until all cutover gates pass, the repo must not publish or retain an unqualified `claim_status: complete`.

## Closure Doctrine
The active release may declare `claim_status: complete` only when:
- blocker ledger is zero,
- all mandatory acceptance gates are green,
- two consecutive certification passes are clean and byte-stable,
- and the generated closure evidence bundle proves TC-01 through TC-10 without contradiction.

If any one of those conditions is false, the correct published status is **not complete**.

## Dependency Ordering
1. **Blocker A** and **Blocker B** are structural prerequisites. They eliminate semantic and authority ambiguity.
2. **Blocker C** and **Blocker D** normalize retained runtime/disclosure evidence.
3. **Blocker E** is the certifying guardrail that prevents future false completion claims.

## Non-Blocking Follow-Ons (after completion, not before)
- richer retirement / ablation receipts,
- broader lab taxonomy cleanup,
- additional support-universe expansion only after new dossiers and proof bundles exist.
