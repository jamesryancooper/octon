# Executive Brief

## Bottom line

Octon is **close enough to the target state to justify finishing the transition**, but **not close enough to claim completion**.

The repository already contains major target-state assets in substance:
- a real constitutional kernel,
- a workspace charter pair,
- a run-rooted control/evidence topology,
- authority contract families and roots,
- host/model adapter manifests,
- a top-level lab domain,
- top-level observability,
- a support-target matrix,
- RunCard and HarnessCard disclosure surfaces,
- build-to-delete governance overlays.

What remains is not cosmetic. The unresolved items sit directly on the critical path of the final claim:
1. the runtime is still not clearly **run-contract-native**;
2. the authority engine is still not clearly **substantive and independent**;
3. durable lifecycle, replay, measurement, intervention, and resumability are still not clearly **routine on the live path**;
4. proof planes are not yet obviously **universal release/promotion gates**;
5. lab is real, but not yet unquestionably **live as an experimentation substrate**;
6. adapter/capability/support-target enforcement still trails the contract shape;
7. disclosure/retention/closeout gates are still too **closure-centric**;
8. legacy shims and historical wave evidence remain overrepresented.

## What this packet does

This packet converts the audit into an actionable completion program. It defines:
- the exact target-state claim Octon is aiming at,
- the exact conditions under which that claim becomes honest,
- the repo paths and subsystems that must change,
- the dependency-ordered workstreams to change them,
- the artifact-level completion criteria for every constitutional contract family,
- the checklists that must turn green before the claim is published.

## Completion doctrine

The governing doctrine is:

> **Do not widen claims before widening truth.**
>
> Do not accept naming, TODOs, empty roots, compatibility shims, closure bundles, or thin abstractions as completion.
> Finish the runtime. Finish authority. Finish proof. Finish disclosure. Then delete what is no longer load-bearing.

## Priority order

1. WS0 — Runtime/objective cutover  
2. WS1 — Authority engine/artifact routing  
3. WS2 — Durable lifecycle/replay/evidence  
4. WS3 — Proof planes + lab runtime  
5. WS4 — Adapters/capability packs/support targets  
6. WS5 — Disclosure/retention/release gates  
7. WS6 — Simplification/deletion/brownfield adoption

## Final judgment encoded as program policy

Octon may claim **fully unified execution constitution** only when all of the following are simultaneously true:
- the live runtime starts from run contracts,
- authority is runtime-consumed and artifact-native,
- consequential runs are durable and replayable,
- proof planes are complete and required,
- lab is first-class in exercised substance,
- support targets are enforced rather than merely declared,
- disclosure/retention are routine and release-blocking,
- obsolete transitional scaffolds are retired or explicitly justified.
