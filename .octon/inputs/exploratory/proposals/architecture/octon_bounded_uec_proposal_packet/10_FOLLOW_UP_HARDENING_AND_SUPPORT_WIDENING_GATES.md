# 10. Follow-up hardening and support-widening gates

## 10.1 Principle

Once the packet target state is reached, the current live universe is hardened and normalized. The remaining follow-up program is therefore **not** about fixing hidden debt left behind by this packet. It is about:

- continuing to deepen evidence quality, and
- controlling future widening.

## 10.2 Ongoing hardening after packet closure

The following continue as standing programs even after bounded closure is reissued:

### H-POST-01 — Evidence corpus deepening
Continue to add:

- more scenario diversity,
- more retained representative runs,
- more locale/adapter combinations within the admitted universe,
- and more replayable negative-path evidence.

### H-POST-02 — Evaluator independence strengthening
Increase:

- hidden-check novelty,
- differential/shadow checks,
- sampled human spot checks,
- and evaluator independence between deterministic and generative validators.

### H-POST-03 — Retirement pressure and deletion readiness
Keep retirement-tracked surfaces under active review so that future deletion is based on evidence rather than inertia.

### H-POST-04 — Ingress simplification beyond packet scope
Any further simplification beyond the manifest-driven model should be evidence-led and may occur after the packet without affecting bounded closure.

### H-POST-05 — Further projection-shell reduction
Even after packet closure, continue reducing workflow-hosted approval and evaluator logic so host surfaces remain thin projection adapters over durable repo-local logic.

### H-POST-06 — Legacy version retirement
Continue retiring stale schema/version declarations and compatibility-era references once lineage needs are gone and active-version coherence is stable.

## 10.3 Future widening blockers

No future widening is allowed unless all of the following blocker groups are satisfied.

### FW-01 — Fresh widening audit
A new widening-specific audit bundle must exist and explicitly evaluate:

- the new tuple(s),
- new adapters or capability packs,
- new locales or contexts,
- and any new authority/non-authority boundary risk.

### FW-02 — Fresh review packet
A new build-to-delete review packet must exist for the widened universe and supersede the prior one.

### FW-03 — Dossier completeness for new tuples
Every newly proposed admitted tuple must have:

- an admission file,
- a support dossier,
- required run evidence,
- naturalistic representative evidence for consequential tuples,
- required scenario coverage,
- evaluator diversity,
- at least one non-host-exclusive evaluator path for consequential tuples,
- hidden-check breadth,
- and release-level coverage entries.

### FW-04 — Non-authority and retirement updates
If widening adds or retains new mirrors, projections, shims, or operator surfaces, they must be entered into the appropriate register before widening can be promoted.

### FW-05 — Disclosure regeneration and dual-pass recertification
All active claim-bearing artifacts must be regenerated and pass dual-pass validation again for the widened universe.

## 10.4 Blockers to broader claim language

Even if support is widened, broader claim wording is still blocked until:

1. active wording remains bounded to the newly admitted finite universe;
2. no “global” or “universal” machine wording returns;
3. historical superseded releases are not cited as current truth;
4. all new tuples satisfy the same evidence-depth and evaluator/hidden-check standards.

## 10.5 Future residual policy

After this packet, the residual ledger should be reserved for:

- future widening blockers,
- true new non-critical hardening observations,
- or regressions discovered after release.

It should no longer carry the six currently retained follow-ups, because this packet closes them.
