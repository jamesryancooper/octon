# Evolution Lifecycle Standards

## Scope

These standards govern Octon's self-evolution proposal-to-promotion lifecycle.
They apply to Evolution Programs, Evolution Candidates, distillation records,
governance impact simulations, lab gates, proposal compilation, Constitutional
Amendment Requests, promotion receipts, recertification, rollback/retirement,
and the Evolution Ledger.

## Lifecycle

1. Retained evidence or recurring friction may produce an
   Evidence-to-Candidate Distillation Record.
2. The distillation record may create an Evolution Candidate with evidence refs,
   impact classifications, required proof, rollback posture, and disposition.
3. Authority-impacting candidates require governance impact simulation.
4. Behavioral or runtime-impacting candidates require lab, replay, scenario,
   shadow, rollback, or negative-control proof appropriate to risk.
5. Viable candidates compile into manifest-governed proposal packets.
6. Authority-changing proposals require Decision Requests, and constitutional
   changes require Constitutional Amendment Requests.
7. Accepted proposals promote only to declared durable targets.
8. Promotion emits retained receipts, refreshes generated projections only as
   derived views, and triggers recertification.
9. Recertification must pass or fail closed before closeout.
10. The Evolution Ledger indexes lineage without replacing source truth.

## Non-Authority Rules

- Evidence suggests change; it does not authorize change.
- Models may propose change; they do not approve change.
- Lab success is not approval.
- Simulation success is not approval.
- Proposal packets are not runtime authority.
- Generated summaries are not authority.
- Evidence distillation is not automatic promotion.
- The Evolution Ledger is an index, not source truth.

## Human / Quorum Gate

Human or quorum approval is required for constitutional changes, support-target
widening, live capability admission, connector live-effectful admission,
release/deployment authority changes, cross-repo policy changes, evidence
obligation changes, authorization-boundary changes, generated/effective trust
changes, and durable promotion of proposal outputs.

## MVP Boundary

The v5 MVP supports one Evolution Program, evidence-backed candidates,
distillation records, governance impact simulation, assurance lab gates,
proposal compiler integration, Constitutional Amendment Request shape,
Promotion Runtime gates, Promotion Receipts, Recertification Results, an
Evolution Ledger, and rollback/retirement posture. It does not implement
autonomous amendments, automatic support widening, connector live admission,
release authority changes, AI-only quorum, or self-modifying runtime behavior.
